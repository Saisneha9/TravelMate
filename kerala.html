Kerala Travel Guide

Discover the enchanting backwaters, pristine beaches, and lush greenery of God's Own Country

Kerala, affectionately known as "God's Own Country," is a narrow strip of land on the southwestern coast of India that offers an extraordinary blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and serene experiences. From the tranquil backwaters and pristine beaches to misty hill stations and lush forests, Kerala captivates travelers with its diverse landscapes, traditional art forms, Ayurvedic treatments, and delicious cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Kerala

Winter (October to February)

Best Season: Perfect weather with pleasant temperatures and minimal rainfall.

Temperature: 22°C to 32°C

Ideal for: Backwater cruises, beach holidays, wildlife safaris, cultural experiences

Events: Kerala Travel Mart (November), Cochin Carnival (December), Theyyam performances

Summer (March to May)

Moderate: Hot and humid in the plains, but pleasant in hill stations.

Temperature: 28°C to 37°C

Ideal for: Hill stations like Munnar and Wayanad, Ayurvedic treatments

Events: Thrissur Pooram (April-May), Vishu celebrations (April)

Monsoon (June to September)

Special Experience: Lush green landscapes with dramatic rainfall.

Temperature: 22°C to 30°C

Ideal for: Ayurvedic treatments, photography, budget travel, fewer tourists

Events: Onam Festival (August-September), Snake Boat Races (August-September)

Recommended Itineraries

Quick Kerala Tour (3-5 Days)

Perfect for short breaks or first-time visitors wanting to experience the highlights.

Day 1-2: Kochi

Explore the historic Fort Kochi area with its Chinese fishing nets, Dutch Palace, Jewish Synagogue, and spice markets. Enjoy a cultural Kathakali performance in the evening.

Day 3: Alleppey

Experience the famous Kerala backwaters with a houseboat cruise. Witness village life along the canals and enjoy fresh seafood prepared on board.

Day 4-5: Munnar

Visit the picturesque hill station with its tea plantations, Eravikulam National Park, and panoramic mountain views.

Classic Kerala Tour (7-10 Days)

A comprehensive tour covering the major attractions across Kerala.

Day 1-2: Kochi

Explore the historic port city with its colonial architecture and cultural attractions.

Day 3-4: Munnar

Discover the misty tea plantations, visit Eravikulam National Park to spot the endangered Nilgiri Tahr.

Day 5: Thekkady

Visit Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, enjoy a boat ride on Periyar Lake, and experience spice plantation tours.

Day 6-7: Alleppey & Kumarakom

Cruise the serene backwaters on a traditional houseboat and relax at Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary.

Day 8: Kovalam

Unwind at the beautiful beaches and enjoy water sports activities.

Day 9-10: Trivandrum

Visit the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Napier Museum, and take a day trip to Kanyakumari (Tamil Nadu).

Complete Kerala Exploration (14+ Days)

An in-depth journey to experience the full diversity of Kerala.

Day 1-3: Kochi & Surroundings

Thoroughly explore Fort Kochi, Mattancherry, and take a day trip to Cherai Beach and the nearby islands.

Day 4-5: Wayanad

Trek through the lush forests, visit ancient caves, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries in the Western Ghats.

Day 6-7: Kozhikode (Calicut)

Experience the historical city with its beaches, food culture, and traditional markets.

Day 8-9: Munnar

Explore the hill station's tea estates, viewpoints, and natural attractions at a relaxed pace.

Day 10-11: Thekkady & Kumily

Enjoy wildlife safaris, eco-tourism activities, and cultural experiences in the Western Ghats.

Day 12-13: Alleppey & Kumarakom

Spend quality time on the backwaters with overnight houseboat stays and village experiences.

Day 14-15: Varkala & Kovalam

Relax at the cliff-backed beaches of Varkala and the palm-lined shores of Kovalam.

Day 16-17: Trivandrum & Kanyakumari

Explore the capital city and take an excursion to the southernmost tip of mainland India.

Essential Travel Tips for Kerala

  • Dress appropriately: Light, breathable clothes for the humid climate. Modest attire when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Monsoon travel: If traveling during monsoon season, carry rain gear and waterproof bags for electronics.
  • Book houseboats in advance: Especially during peak season (December-January), backwater cruises get fully booked.
  • Try the local cuisine: Kerala's seafood, coconut-based curries, and traditional sadhya (feast) are must-try experiences.
  • Transportation: Use the efficient public transport system or hire private cars for hill station visits.
  • Mosquito protection: Carry insect repellent, especially for coastal and backwater areas.
  • Language: Malayalam is the local language, but English is widely understood in tourist areas.
  • Ayurvedic treatments: Book authentic experiences at certified centers rather than tourist traps.

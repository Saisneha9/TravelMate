Sikkim Travel Guide

Discover the serene beauty, Buddhist monasteries, and majestic Himalayas of the Land of Orchids

Sikkim, known as the "Land of Orchids," is India's second smallest state and a pristine Himalayan wonderland. From soaring mountain peaks and sacred lakes to ancient monasteries and rich biodiversity, Sikkim offers a unique blend of natural beauty, spiritual heritage, and cultural diversity that captivates travelers seeking tranquility and adventure.

Best Time to Visit Sikkim

Spring (March to May)

Best Season: Perfect weather with blooming flowers and clear mountain views.

Temperature: 5°C to 25°C

Ideal for: Trekking, rhododendron festivals, monastery visits

Events: Saga Dawa (May-June), Flower Exhibition (April-May)

Autumn (September to November)

Excellent: Clear skies with spectacular mountain views.

Temperature: 10°C to 20°C

Ideal for: Trekking, photography, cultural experiences

Events: Dashain Festival (October), Tihar (November)

Monsoon (June to August)

Challenging: Heavy rainfall with occasional landslides.

Temperature: 15°C to 25°C

Ideal for: Budget travelers, lush green landscapes, fewer tourists

Events: Drukpa Tseshi (July), Pang Lhabsol (August)

Winter (December to February)

Cold but Beautiful: Snow-covered landscapes with festive atmosphere.

Temperature: -5°C to 10°C

Ideal for: Snow experiences, winter festivals, lower altitude areas

Events: Losoong Festival (December), Losar (Tibetan New Year, February)

Recommended Itineraries

Quick Sikkim Tour (3-5 Days)

Perfect for short breaks or first-time visitors wanting to experience the highlights.

Day 1-2: Gangtok

Start your journey in the capital city. Visit Rumtek Monastery, Enchey Monastery, MG Marg, and Nathula Pass (permit required). Explore local markets for handicrafts and organic products.

Day 3: Tsomgo Lake & Baba Mandir

Visit the sacred Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake) and pay respects at Baba Mandir. Enjoy yak rides and stunning Himalayan landscapes.

Day 4-5: Pelling

Experience breathtaking views of Kanchenjunga. Visit Pemayangtse Monastery, Rabdentse Ruins, Khecheopalri Lake, and Kanchenjunga Falls.

Classic Sikkim Tour (7-10 Days)

A comprehensive tour covering the major attractions and regions.

Day 1-3: Gangtok & East Sikkim

Explore the capital and surrounding attractions including Rumtek Monastery, Nathula Pass, Tsomgo Lake, Baba Mandir, and Tashi View Point.

Day 4-5: Pelling & West Sikkim

Visit Pemayangtse Monastery, Rabdentse Ruins, Khecheopalri Lake, Kanchenjunga Falls, and enjoy Kanchenjunga views.

Day 6-7: Ravangla & South Sikkim

Explore Buddha Park, Temi Tea Garden, Borong Hot Springs, and Namchi's Char Dham and Samdruptse.

Day 8-9: Lachung & North Sikkim

Journey to North Sikkim to see Yumthang Valley (Valley of Flowers), Zero Point, and Lachung Monastery.

Day 10: Return to Gangtok

Last-minute shopping and relaxation in Gangtok before departure.

Complete Sikkim Exploration (14+ Days)

An in-depth journey to experience the full diversity of Sikkim.

Day 1-3: Gangtok & East Sikkim

Thoroughly explore the capital and surroundings, including Rumtek Monastery, Nathula Pass, Tsomgo Lake, Baba Mandir, Hanuman Tok, and Ganesh Tok.

Day 4-6: Lachung & Yumthang Valley

Enjoy North Sikkim's beauty with visits to Yumthang Valley, Zero Point, and Lachung Monastery. Experience local Lepcha-Bhutia culture.

Day 7-8: Lachen & Gurudongmar Lake

Visit remote North Sikkim including Lachen village, Thangu, and Gurudongmar Lake (one of India's highest lakes).

Day 9-11: Pelling & West Sikkim

Explore West Sikkim's monasteries, lakes, and waterfalls. Optional trek to Dzongri for closer Kanchenjunga views.

Day 12-13: Ravangla & Namchi

Discover South Sikkim's spiritual sites, tea gardens, and hot springs.

Day 14-15: Zuluk & Silk Route

Follow the historic Silk Route with stays in Zuluk, Lungthung, and Gnathang Valley. Enjoy stunning Eastern Himalayan landscapes.

Day 16: Return to Gangtok

Final shopping, relaxation, and cultural experiences in Gangtok before departure.

Essential Travel Tips for Sikkim

  • Inner Line Permits: Foreign nationals and Indian tourists require permits for certain areas. Arrange these in advance through a registered travel agency.
  • Altitude sickness: Acclimatize properly when visiting high-altitude areas like Nathula Pass and Gurudongmar Lake.
  • Respect monasteries: Remove shoes, avoid photographing inside prayer halls without permission, and dress modestly.
  • Weather preparedness: Pack layers and rainwear regardless of season due to unpredictable mountain weather.
  • Transportation: Hire a local driver familiar with mountain roads. Shared jeeps are available between major towns.
  • Connectivity: Mobile network and internet can be unreliable in remote areas. Download offline maps.
  • Eco-conscious travel: Sikkim is an organic state. Avoid single-use plastics and respect nature conservation efforts.
  • Language: Nepali, Bhutia, Lepcha, and Hindi are commonly spoken. English is understood in tourist areas.
  • Book in advance: During peak seasons (April-May and October-November), book accommodations well in advance.

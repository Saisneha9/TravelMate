Uttarakhand Travel Guide

Discover the spiritual essence, majestic Himalayas, and pristine wilderness of the Land of Gods

Uttarakhand, known as "Devbhumi" or the Land of Gods, is a mountainous state in northern India renowned for its stunning Himalayan landscapes, sacred pilgrimage sites, and abundant natural beauty. From ancient temples and snow-capped peaks to pristine lakes and rich biodiversity, Uttarakhand offers a perfect blend of spiritual experiences, adventure activities, and tranquil retreats that attract travelers seeking both excitement and solace.

Best Time to Visit Uttarakhand

Spring (March to May)

Best Season: Pleasant weather with blooming flowers and clear mountain views.

Temperature: 10°C to 25°C

Ideal for: Valley of Flowers (late period), trekking, sightseeing, pilgrimage

Events: Holi, Basant Panchami, Phool Dei (flower festival)

Summer (June to August)

Moderate: Pleasant in hills, ideal escape from plains heat.

Temperature: 15°C to 30°C

Ideal for: Hill stations, Char Dham Yatra, Valley of Flowers

Events: Ganga Dussehra (June), Nanda Devi Raj Jat (August, once every 12 years)

Autumn/Winter (September to February)

Varied: Clear views in autumn, snow in winter.

Temperature: -5°C to 15°C

Ideal for: Photography, skiing (Dec-Feb), wildlife viewing

Events: Kumbh Mela (periodic), Uttarayani Fair (January), winter skiing festivals

Recommended Itineraries

Quick Uttarakhand Tour (3-5 Days)

Perfect for weekends or short breaks to experience the mountain beauty.

Day 1-2: Mussoorie & Dehradun

Begin your journey in Dehradun. Visit Robber's Cave, FRI, and drive to Mussoorie. Explore Mall Road, Kempty Falls, and enjoy sunset at Gun Hill.

Day 3: Rishikesh

Experience the spiritual side with visits to temples and ashrams. Participate in Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat and enjoy adventure activities like river rafting.

Day 4-5: Haridwar & Nearby

Explore the holy city of Haridwar. Witness the evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri, visit Mansa Devi Temple, and take a day trip to Rajaji National Park.

Classic Uttarakhand Tour (7-10 Days)

A comprehensive tour covering major attractions and experiences.

Day 1-2: Rishikesh & Haridwar

Explore these spiritual towns, participate in river rafting, and attend the evening Ganga Aarti ceremonies.

Day 3-4: Mussoorie

Visit the Queen of Hills with its colonial charm, stunning viewpoints, and nearby attractions like Kempty Falls.

Day 5-6: Nainital

Enjoy boat rides on Naini Lake, visit nearby viewpoints, and explore Naina Devi Temple.

Day 7: Jim Corbett National Park

Go on wildlife safaris to spot tigers, elephants, and various bird species in India's oldest national park.

Day 8-9: Almora & Kausani

Experience the cultural heartland of Kumaon with stunning Himalayan views, especially the panoramic view of Nanda Devi.

Day 10: Ranikhet

Visit this peaceful hill station developed by the British with beautiful apple orchards and pine forests.

Complete Uttarakhand Exploration (14+ Days)

An in-depth journey to experience the diverse landscapes and spirituality of Uttarakhand.

Day 1-3: Haridwar & Rishikesh

Begin your spiritual journey at these sacred towns along the Ganges, with yoga sessions, temple visits, and adventure activities.

Day 4-5: Mussoorie & Dehradun

Explore the colonial charm of Mussoorie and the vibrant city of Dehradun with its institutions and markets.

Day 6-8: Char Dham Yatra (Part 1)

Begin the sacred pilgrimage with visits to Yamunotri and Gangotri temples, the sources of sacred rivers.

Day 9-11: Char Dham Yatra (Part 2)

Continue to Kedarnath and Badrinath, two of the most sacred Hindu temples in the Himalayas.

Day 12-13: Valley of Flowers & Hemkund Sahib

Trek to the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Valley of Flowers (seasonal) and visit the sacred Sikh pilgrimage site of Hemkund Sahib.

Day 14-15: Nainital & Bhimtal

Relax at these picturesque lake towns in the Kumaon region with boating and nature walks.

Day 16-17: Jim Corbett & Kaladhungi

Experience wildlife safaris and visit Jim Corbett's winter home and museum.

Day 18: Almora & Kausani

Explore these cultural towns with magnificent Himalayan views and local crafts.

Essential Travel Tips for Uttarakhand

  • Altitude awareness: Acclimatize properly when visiting high-altitude destinations to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Weather preparation: Pack layers regardless of season as mountain weather can change rapidly.
  • Respect sacred sites: Dress modestly when visiting temples and follow local customs and traditions.
  • Trekking safety: For trekking, carry proper gear, inform authorities of your routes, and consider hiring local guides.
  • Wildlife guidelines: Maintain distance from wild animals and follow all safety rules in national parks.
  • Transportation: Book shared taxis or private vehicles for mountain journeys, as public transport can be limited in remote areas.
  • Eco-friendly practices: Carry back all non-biodegradable waste from trekking and camping areas.
  • Pilgrim season: For Char Dham Yatra (April-November), book accommodations months in advance due to high demand.
